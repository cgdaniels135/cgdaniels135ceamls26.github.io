project_title: "Optimization Driven Machine Learning Framework for Predicting Respiratory Health Risks Using Urban Environmental Data: Baltimore as a Case Study"

problem: |
  Briefly describe the problem your project tackles. Who is affected? Why does this
  matter? What gap or limitation are you trying to address?

  Respiratory diseases such as asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and other breathing-related illnesses are strongly influenced by     environmental conditions. Residents in urban areas like Baltimore are particularly vulnerable because they are exposed to varying levels of air pollution and changing weather conditions. These health issues can lead to increased hospital visits, reduced quality of life, and greater healthcare costs.

approach: |
  Describe how you'll approach the problem. A bulleted plan works well:

  - Step 1 — Data Collection & Preparation
  - Step 2 — Model Development
  - Step 3 — Evaluation & Validation
  - Step 4 — Communication of Results

  The tools we will be using are Python, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, XGBoost, optimization techniques for feature selection and hyperparameter tuning, and environmental and health datasets from Baltimore.

outcome: |
  What do you expect to produce by the end of the program? A working prototype?
  A research poster? A short paper? Describe the artifacts and what you hope
  others will be able to do with them.

  By the end of the program, we expect to develop a working machine learning framework capable of predicting respiratory health risks using urban environmental data from Baltimore. The framework will demonstrate how optimization techniques can improve prediction accuracy and identify the environmental factors most strongly associated with respiratory disease occurrences.

  In addition to the predictive model, the project will produce a research poster, data visualizations, and a short research paper summarizing the methodology and findings. These outcomes will provide researchers and public health professionals with insights that may support early warning systems and data-driven strategies for reducing respiratory health risks in urban communities.
