what_i_learned: |
  Today, my team and I focused on filming and editing the promotional video for our research project. We dedicated a significant amount of time to capturing the footage, ensuring we had enough material to effectively communicate our ideas. Looking back on the recording session, I realized it would have been better to shoot the entire video in landscape mode to maintain a consistent orientation throughout. Despite that small detail, I think the final video came out incredibly well and looks quite polished. The editing phase turned out to be a long and meticulous process, requiring a lot of patience and focus. A major part of the post-production work involved carefully reviewing our media assets to decide which images and video clips to use as overlays. Selecting the right visuals was crucial for adding context and keeping the audience engaged during the presentation. Overall, it was a highly productive day that brought us much closer to having a complete media package for our upcoming symposium.

reflection: |
  Recording and editing the promotional video for our research project turned out to be an incredibly rewarding and dynamic experience. While the editing phase required a lot of patience to sift through various media assets and choose the perfect video and image overlays, the spontaneous creativity of the production kept the momentum going. Coming up with unexpected ideas on the fly and immediately finding clever ways to implement them into our footage made the entire process feel genuinely fun and engaging. Even though a look back at the footage taught us a valuable lesson about sticking strictly to landscape mode for a consistent orientation, the minor technical adjustment didn't take away from how polished the final product feels. Ultimately, this filming session allowed us to balance the structured demands of our project with a welcome dose of creative freedom, turning a long post-production effort into a highly enjoyable highlight of our symposium preparation.
