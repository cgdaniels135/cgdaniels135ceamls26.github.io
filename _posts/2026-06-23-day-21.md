what_i_learned: |
  Today, I expanded my data analysis skills in Python by learning how to sort complex datasets, which is an essential step in organizing and making sense of raw information. I am applying this skill to a dataset focused on the respiratory cancer risks in Baltimore City and Baltimore County, evaluating how these risks align with various potential contributing factors. By sorting this data, I can now easily identify neighborhoods or tracts with the highest and lowest risks, allowing me to pinpoint critical geographic trends and disparities. Additionally, I learned how to interpret box plots and truly understand the story behind the visualization. I can now confidently break down a box plot to identify the median, the upper and lower quartiles, and the overall spread of the data. This visualization technique is incredibly useful because it allows me to see the distribution of respiratory risks across different areas at a single glance. Furthermore, understanding box plots helps me detect outliers, which might represent communities facing disproportionately high environmental or health burdens. Combining data sorting with box plot analysis gives me a much stronger foundation for extracting meaningful insights from my public health research. Overall, today's lessons bridged the gap between raw data manipulation and advanced visual interpretation, moving me closer to effectively modeling these health risks.
