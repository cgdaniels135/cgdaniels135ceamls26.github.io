what_i_learned: |
  Today, I focused on a comprehensive machine learning workflow by first splitting my dataset into training and testing sets to ensure proper evaluation. To address potential class imbalance, I applied SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) exclusively to the training data. Next, I trained three distinct types of predictive models: a Random Forest, a Support Vector Machine (SVM), and an Extreme Learning Machine (ELM). I then rigorously evaluated these models using standard classification metrics, including accuracy, precision, recall, and the F1-score. Additionally, I analyzed the ROC-AUC to understand their performance across various classification thresholds and generated a confusion matrix for a detailed breakdown of correct and incorrect predictions. Beyond traditional classification metrics, I measured Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) and Mean Absolute Error (MAE) to assess the models' residual variations. Computational efficiency was also a priority, so I tracked and evaluated the CPU time required for each model's training and execution. To guarantee the generalizability of the results, I carefully checked for overfitting by comparing the training metrics against the testing metrics. Ultimately, after thoroughly examining all performance and efficiency factors, I successfully retained all three trained models for future analysis or deployment.
